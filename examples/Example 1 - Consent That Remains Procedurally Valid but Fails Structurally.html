This case is a bounded PMS-SEX structural reading. It does not diagnose persons, infer hidden motives, rank identities, evaluate legal validity, evaluate person-adultness, or prescribe action. It reconstructs a configuration in which a formally consent-based frame remains procedurally intact while cost distribution, temporality, practical distance-capacity, and asymmetry-specific self-binding strain structurally.

  • Χ (Distance): maintained meta-position and stop-capability; no fusion into accusation, urgency, enforcement, or person-level attribution.

1.3 PMS-SEX-specific guards

2) Minimal Vignette

2.1 Scene-bound vignette

Two adult role-positions enter a formally agreed, private, non-binding sexual interaction. The agreement is not later negated; instead, it becomes the repeated closure device through which hesitation, tonal unease, and after-scene clarification are treated as already settled. Role A can point back to procedural validity and keep the scene narratively closed, while Role B carries stronger exposure, body-boundary, symbolic, exit, and after-scene dignity costs. The structural failure does not lie in absent consent, but in the way consent becomes too heavy a proof-form: speaking later about cost begins to look like reopening, contradiction, accusation, or failure to honor the frame.

2.2 Temporal boundary

The scene begins with the explicit pre-scene agreement, includes the interaction and the repeated handling of hesitation through reference to that agreement, and ends with the after-scene exchange in which later meta-commentary becomes socially or practically costly even though the formal consent record remains intact.
