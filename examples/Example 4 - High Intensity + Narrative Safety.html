2) Minimal Vignette

2.1 Scene-bound and non-identifying vignette

A repeated high-intensity sexual configuration develops over a short-to-medium timeframe. The scene is narrated as safe because communication is frequent, transparent, and mutually reassuring. Under high activation, however, the frame remains verbally stable while practical stop-capability, pacing, recovery realism, cost-topology tracking, and after-cost regulation weaken. Costs appear sudden only after repetition, asymmetry, and temporality have already compressed.

2.7 Observed realizations

  • Frequent reassurance talk replaces structural checks.
  • De-escalation is framed as breaking the mood.
  • Post-hoc explanation substitutes for pre-hoc regulation.
  • Stop-capability exists in principle but is rarely activated under peak pressure.
  • The communicative frame remains narratively stable while recovery and exposure gradients sharpen.
  • High activation makes small coordination gaps consequential before they are nameable.
  • The frame’s internal safety narrative remains stronger than its operational pacing capacity.

10) Adult Sexual Reciprocity and Asymmetry-Specific Responsibility

10.1 Adult sexual reciprocity principle

Adult sexual reciprocity does not require equal burdens. It requires that unequal burdens are not externalized.

10.2 Case application

In this case, adult sexual reciprocity would not require equal tolerance for intensity, equal recovery speed, or identical after-costs. It would require that those unequal burdens remain nameable, that high activation does not override Χ, that post-hoc communication does not replace containment, and that each role-position carries the self-binding made necessary by its own escalation capacity, exposure level, recovery profile, and externalization risk.

22) Structural Closure

22.1 Main closure

High intensity does not create drift by itself. It reveals whether a frame can actually carry what it narrates. When activation is extreme, repetition accelerates, asymmetries sharpen, and time compresses. If regulation remains rhetorical while enactment accelerates, costs do not rise gradually; they jump. What looks like a sudden collapse is often Θ catching up with a frame that never slowed the scene.

22.2 Adult reciprocity closure

The relevant reciprocity question is not whether both roles wanted the intensity, communicated frequently, or narrated the scene as safe. It is whether unequal recovery, exposure, after-cost, exit, and responsibility burdens are externalized. Mutuality becomes drift where it exits cost responsibility; overload becomes drift where it becomes an unmarked lever; safety becomes drift where it becomes authority rather than self-binding.

22.3 Citable sentence

Intensity is a drift multiplier when it converts small coordination gaps into irreversible trajectories faster than Distance (Χ) can regulate them.
