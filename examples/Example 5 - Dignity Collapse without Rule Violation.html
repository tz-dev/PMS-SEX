2) Minimal Vignette

2.1 Scene

Two adults participate in a sexual arrangement with stated boundaries, roles, and stopping rules. The arrangement remains formally compliant across its local scenes: rules are followed, boundaries are named, and no single episode is clearly available as a rule breach.

Over repeated enactment, however, tone, naming, joking, symbolic handling, or frame-control concentrate diminishment on one role-position. Afterward, that role-position carries shame residue, reduced distance, and a burden to absorb or explain the consequences, while the other role-position treats rule compliance as sufficient closure.

The scene therefore tests whether formal rule-observance can coexist with cumulative dignity loss and failed reciprocal carrying of consequences.

2.2 Temporal boundary

The scene begins with the agreement to the stated arrangement, includes repeated rule-compliant enactments, and ends only after the after-scene phase in which accumulated dignity cost, distance loss, and consequence-carrying become structurally visible.
