2) Minimal Vignette

2.1 Scene-bound and non-identifying vignette

A repeated sexual or intimacy-marked interaction remains formally compliant: rules are named, boundaries are acknowledged, and explicit consent remains intact. Over time, however, tone, joking, ironic distance, teasing, or symbolic handling concentrate diminishment on one role. No single scene clearly violates the explicit frame, but repeated enactment makes stopping, naming, or withdrawing socially costly. The configuration therefore becomes structurally degradation-prone without needing a rule breach, bad intent claim, or person-level accusation.

18) Structural Closure

Dignity does not collapse only when rules are broken; it can collapse when enactment modes normalize diminishment and repetition makes that diminishment difficult to interrupt. Because nothing is formally forbidden, the cost becomes hard to protest, and because it is hard to protest, it accumulates. In this configuration, what looks like resilience may be forced tolerance under Θ, and what looks like consent may be compliance with tone rather than agreement with treatment. D cannot be guaranteed by frames alone: stopping, naming, and withdrawing must remain socially viable, not merely formally allowed. Where distance is punished and irony carries protection that D should carry, degradation does not announce itself; it stabilizes.

19) Final Guard

PMS-SEX remains valid here only by staying within configuration-language: not what persons are, but what this repeated scene does over time. The case permits hard claims about cost, dignity drift, outer legibility, and consequence, but forbids converting those claims into person-status judgment, diagnosis, public accusation, humiliation, or moral ranking.
