2) Minimal Vignette

A repeated sexual configuration is framed as ongoing but explicitly non-binding: no exclusivity, no future claim, and no identity docking. Over time, absence, delay, or signs of parallel involvement begin to carry more weight than the frame officially acknowledges. Availability and prioritization become implicitly expected while the frame continues to describe itself as limited, open, and non-possessive.

18) Structural Closure

Non-normative: true

When binding is suspended but continuity persists, structure can bind where language refuses to. Repetition stabilizes expectation, asymmetry channels control, and time turns informal reliance into fact. Possession does not begin here as a declared claim; it begins as a trajectory that cannot be interrupted without cost. By the time conflict appears, binding may already be structurally operative, not by agreement, but by accumulated coordination under denied Ψ. This is not the betrayal of a frame. It is the configuration effect that appears when frames do not carry the structures they generate. Suspended binding does not become unstable because persons want more; it becomes unstable when distance is delegitimized while time keeps running.
