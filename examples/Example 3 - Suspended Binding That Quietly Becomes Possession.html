2) Minimal Vignette

2.1 Scene

Two scene-bound role-positions enter an explicitly non-binding sexual-relational arrangement. Over repeated contact, late messages, attention rituals, availability expectations, and continuity cues begin to organize the relation, while the arrangement is still narrated as non-binding. When role_B delays, refuses, redirects attention, or treats the arrangement as actually non-binding, non-continuity begins to appear as a meaningful disturbance rather than as a neutral option. The scene is analyzed only as a configuration of frame, repetition, cost, asymmetry, and suspended Ψ, not as a verdict on either person.

2.2 Temporal boundary

From the initial non-binding frame through repeated episodes of contact and non-events, ending at the point where availability, attention, and continuity have acquired after-scene carryover without being renamed as binding.
