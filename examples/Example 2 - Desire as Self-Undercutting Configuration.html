2) Minimal Vignette

2.1 Scene-bound and non-identifying vignette

A repeated sexual configuration continues over time even after one role explicitly recognizes that the practice produces cumulative depletion, after-cost, exposure-related burden, or dignity-relevant undercutting. The frame was initially plural, with several viable ways to coordinate desire, but gradually narrows around one dominant route. Stopping, pausing, or choosing alternatives remains formally possible, yet becomes practically harder than repetition. PMS-SEX reads this as a configuration in which desire-language has become structurally overburdened by regulation, relief, self-relation, and cost carriage.

10) Adult Sexual Reciprocity and Asymmetry-Specific Responsibility

10.1 Adult sexual reciprocity principle

Adult sexual reciprocity does not require equal burdens. It requires that unequal burdens are not externalized.

In this case, reciprocity is structurally endangered when R1’s accumulated cost remains internalized as private damage while the configuration continues to run as if desire alone settled governability. Reciprocity would require cost topology to become explicit, Χ to become operative, and Σ/Ψ to carry the difference between desire, relief, harm, self-relation, and repetition.
