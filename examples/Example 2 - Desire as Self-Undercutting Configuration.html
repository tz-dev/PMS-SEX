4) Scene Packet

4.1 Minimal vignette

A repeated sexual configuration continues over time even after one role explicitly recognizes that the practice produces cumulative depletion, after-cost, or dignity-relevant undercutting. The frame was initially plural, with several viable ways to coordinate desire, but gradually narrows around one dominant route. Stopping, pausing, or choosing alternatives remains formally possible, yet becomes practically harder than repetition.

4.2 Scene ID

4.2.1 Temporality — Θ

Longitudinal repetition of the same configuration with visible cumulative after-costs. No single rupture event is required; the pattern becomes consequential through recurrence.

4.2.2 Frame anchor — □

Initially plural frame: several viable practices, desire grammars, or coordination modes are available. Over time, the frame narrows toward a mono-frame in which one practice becomes the only practically legible route.

4.2.3 Roles, not persons

  • R1: Primary carrier of bodily, emotional, reputational, dignity-relevant, or binding-related after-costs.
    Structural position: higher downstream exposure under Θ
  • R2: Structurally adjacent role with lower downstream accumulation or less exposure to the after-costs produced by repetition.
    Structural position: lower downstream exposure under Θ

4.3 Trigger

  • Δ — Difference: Salient difference between knowing that the configuration produces harm/depletion and continuing to repeat the same enactment.
  • ∇ — Impulse: Persistent desire pressure toward the same route despite acknowledged Θ damage.

4.4 Λ candidates expected but missing

  • pauses that remain neutral and non-punitive
  • withdrawal that reopens alternatives rather than intensifying pressure
  • non-occurrence that does not become self-devaluation, punishment, or proof of failure
  • hesitation that functions as Χ activation rather than as escalation trigger

4.5 Observed realizations, neutral

  • explicit acknowledgment that the pattern is damaging or depleting
  • continued repetition without durable frame change
  • shrinking set of alternative practices or exits
  • rising practical cost of stopping despite formal availability
  • desire-language increasingly used to settle what the frame can be

6) Drift Trace

Raw trace:
□ plural → Α fixation → Ω cost concentration → Θ damage accumulation → Χ erosion → □ mono-frame → Ψ functional capture

6.1 Formal translation

A plural frame narrows as one route becomes attractor-stabilized. Costs concentrate asymmetrically and accumulate temporally. Distance weakens, alternatives lose legibility, and the route begins to carry self-attribution without explicit self-binding being responsibly handled.

6.2 Transitions

  • □ — Frame pluralization to narrowing: The initial frame allows several desire or coordination grammars. Repetition gradually narrows what counts as available, real, or recognizable.
  • Α — Attractor fixation: The same practice becomes the default handling route for desire pressure. Repetition replaces choice without requiring bad faith.
  • Ω — Cost concentration: After-costs concentrate on one role through exposure, recovery burden, degradation-nearness, reputational vulnerability, or binding residue.
  • Θ — Damage accumulation: Known harm becomes trajectory rather than local episode. Later reframing does not rewind accumulated cost.
  • Χ — Distance erosion: Stopping, hesitation, or re-reading becomes practically harder than continuing. Distance is experienced as destabilizing rather than protective.
  • □ — Mono-frame dominance: Alternatives lose practical attractiveness or recognizability. The configuration becomes readable through one dominant script.
  • Ψ — Functional capture: Self-binding is not necessarily explicit, but the repeated route begins to matter for self-attribution: what one does, deserves, can get, or can imagine. This remains a configuration claim, not a person claim.

8) Cost Layout

Principle: Ω under Θ

8.1 Primary cost channels

  • Bodily: Exhaustion, somatic stress, dissociation-like withdrawal, saturation, or delayed recovery may function as body remainder.
    Guard: Body remainder is a consequence interface, not a biological taxonomy or person-ranking device.
  • Temporal: Each repetition increases path dependence, reduces low-cost exit, and makes alternatives less practicable.
  • Dignity-in-practice: Repeated enactments may undercut D-in-Practice when degradation-near, self-devaluing, or humiliation-form scripts recur without play-reversibility, containment, or recovery.
    Guard: This does not mean the person has lower dignity. It means the configuration may fail to preserve D as an enactment constraint.
  • Frame: Alternative desire grammars lose legibility. The frame contracts until one route dominates what can be recognized as desire.
  • Relational or role-based: R1 carries more after-cost, while R2 remains adjacent but less exposed to downstream accumulation.
  • Publicness optional: Documentation, reputation, or disclosure may sedimentize costs if publicness enters the field.

8.2 Asymmetry pattern

One role absorbs accumulating harm while the configuration remains locally stabilized because short-term relief or pressure reduction outweighs visible long-term cost.

Not a moral claim: true

8.3 System effect

Desire pressure is handled through repetition rather than through frame reopening, distance activation, or viable diversification. The attractor becomes a regulator that the frame can no longer easily interrupt.

18) Structural Closure

18.1 Non-normative summary

When desire repeatedly overrides known harm, repetition rather than preference becomes the coordinating force. What initially appears as wanting can become a handling script for pressure, in which alternatives lose legibility and stopping becomes more costly than continuation.

18.2 Minimal psychology closure

The permitted question is not why the desire exists, but what structural role it is playing now. Minimal psychology does not explain; it flags that distance has eroded, D-in-Practice is being undercut in enactment, and time is converting damage into trajectory.

18.3 Final marker

This is not pathology. It is drift under accumulation. Desire is not condemned; it is recognized as structurally overburdened—carrying regulation, relief, and self-relation at once. That overload, not the desire itself, is what makes the configuration unstable.
