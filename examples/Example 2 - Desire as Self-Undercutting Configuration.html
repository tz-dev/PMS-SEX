3) Minimal Vignette

3.1 Scene

A recurring intimate desire-script continues to define what counts as the desirable scene. At first, the script draws its force from conditions such as responsiveness, openness, novelty, mutual readability, and the sense that the other or the situation remains more than the script. Under repetition, however, the desire no longer merely returns; it begins to require that the scene reproduce the conditions under which the desire first became compelling.

The pattern therefore shifts from attraction to enactment grammar: anticipation becomes scripted, direct otherness weakens, interruption starts to look like a threat to desire itself, and after-scene residues accumulate because the conditions of desirability are being consumed by the very repetition that still invokes them.

3.2 Temporal boundary

The scene begins when the recurring desire-pattern again becomes the organizing frame and ends when the repeated enactment has made interruption, re-reading, or condition-renewal structurally more costly than continuation.

The scene may initially appear as stable preference, repeated attraction, familiar intimacy, or a reliable desire-pattern.

18.1 Summary

The case reads a recurring desire-pattern as a scene-bound configuration in which desire remains internally real while its repeated enactment grammar consumes the conditions that made it desirable. The dominant operator profile is ∇-Α-Θ/Χ-Ψ: activation returns through the same script, the script becomes easier to reproduce than to re-read, temporal cost accumulates, interruption becomes symbolically expensive, and self-binding is required to carry the contradiction without externalizing condition erosion.

Cost topology is partial rather than score-like: the decisive costs are condition-renewal costs, interruption costs, symbolic continuity costs, after-scene residue, and responsibility for recurrence.

Dignity-in-Practice is strained only where the weakening conditions are denied, externalized, or made practically uninterruptible; recurring desire by itself is not a D-failure. The minimal psychology adapter may activate as a structural alarm because PMS-SEX permits the question of what structural role desire plays only as a probe of pattern continuity, not as motive theory or diagnosis.
