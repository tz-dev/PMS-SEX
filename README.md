# PMS–SEX

## From Impulse to Self-Binding: A Praxeological Grammar of Sexuality (Δ–Ψ)

**PMS–SEX** is a domain-specific high-asymmetry application overlay of the **Praxeological Meta-Structure Theory (PMS)**. It applies the canonical PMS operator grammar (Δ–Ψ) to the structural problem of **sexual praxis**: how impulse, framing, non-events, repetition, asymmetry, temporality, distance, integration, and self-binding produce consequences over time.

PMS–SEX does **not** modify, extend, reinterpret, or replace the PMS operator set. It is an operator-strict overlay that uses PMS as its structural foundation.

Its central thesis is:

> Sexuality does not become structurally consequential because it is immoral.  
> It becomes consequential because it occurs under bodies, frames, asymmetry, repetition, non-events, and time.

Where many approaches to sexuality default to psychology, morality, diagnosis, taxonomy, identity ranking, or advice, PMS–SEX stays strictly praxeological:

```text
impulse
→ frame
→ expectation / non-event
→ repetition
→ asymmetry
→ temporal carryover
→ recontextualization
→ distance or drift
→ integration or fragmentation
→ self-binding
```

## 4. Core Thesis

PMS–SEX argues that sexual practice becomes structurally readable because:

- **Λ / Non-Event** is active: absence, withdrawal, hesitation, delay, or non-occurrence can steer under expectation.
- **Α / Attractor** stabilizes: repetition consolidates scripts and narrows options.
- **Ω / Asymmetry** distributes costs: access, exposure, obligation, exit margin, and repair load rarely remain symmetric.
- **Θ / Temporality** accumulates: consequences persist and reshape exit realism over time.
- **Φ / Recontextualization** can reframe a scene, but does not rewind accumulated structure.
- **Χ / Distance** is the decisive limiter: without practical stop-capability and meta-position, drift becomes hard to intercept.
- **Σ / Integration** is required where contradictory demands must be held without denial or coercive simplification.
- **Ψ / Self-Binding** is a threshold: local events and non-events can become biography-relevant, binding-relevant, or identity-relevant under time.

The compact formulation is:

> Sexuality does not “cost” because it is immoral.  
> It costs because it happens under bodies, frames, asymmetry, repetition, and time.

## 9. Inner / Outer / Public Layer Discipline

PMS–SEX distinguishes four layers that may overlap but must not be collapsed.

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Internal framing | How is the scene inhabited, narrated, or lived from within? |
| Outer legibility | What action-form is structurally readable under asymmetry, exposure, repetition, and consequence? |
| Publicness | Does visibility, documentation, reputation, or circulation amplify consequences? |
| Temporal carryover | How does the scene continue across Θ? |

Publicness is treated as a **□ extension**.

It may:

- distribute consequences,
- amplify consequences,
- document consequences,
- repeat consequences,
- sedimentize consequences,
- and increase exposure.

But publicness does not necessarily generate the primary action-form.

## 10. Structural Contributions

### 10.1 Sexuality as Consequence System

PMS–SEX reconstructs sexuality as praxis that produces:

- scripts and attractors (Α),
- cost gradients (Ω),
- trajectory effects (Θ),
- recontextualization pressure (Φ),
- distance or stop-capability stress (Χ),
- integration demands (Σ),
- binding relevance (Ψ).

It is a consequence system, not a morality field.

### 10.2 Non-Event Seriousness

Withdrawal, silence, delay, hesitation, refusal, absence, and non-occurrence are treated as structurally active under expectation.

They are not simply “nothing happened.”

Under Λ, non-events may affect:

- expectation,
- role,
- trajectory,
- cost,
- exit realism,
- binding,
- and dignity-in-practice.

### 10.3 Viability without Moralizing

Functional practice is defined by **governability**, not normality.

A structurally governable configuration requires:

```text
□ explicit
+ Ω nameable
+ Θ realistic
+ Χ operative
+ Λ tolerable
+ Ψ handled
(+ Σ reachable)
= structurally governable configuration
```

This is not a moral score and not a person evaluation.

### 10.4 Outer Legibility without Person-Ranking

PMS–SEX can name externally readable action-forms such as:

- asymmetry-form,
- exposure-form,
- humiliation-form,
- degradation-form,
- possession-form,
- binding-form,
- exit-cost form.

But naming outer legibility must not become:

- moral verdict,
- identity judgment,
- diagnosis,
- orientation ranking,
- person-worth claim,
- or sanction logic.

### 10.5 Path-Cost without Pathology

PMS–SEX may describe higher stabilization burden, disturbance, or path-cost only at the level of configuration dynamics.

The compact rule is:

> Disturbance names structural stabilization burden, not human worth.

### 10.6 Frame Thesis

Situation and frame (□) often override later intention-description.

This does not mean intention is irrelevant.

It means:

```text
internal meaning
≠ automatic structural reversal
```

A later Φ can recontextualize a scene, but it cannot erase Θ accumulation.

### 10.7 Exit Realism

Exit changes trajectory but does not reset accumulated structure.

In PMS–SEX, exit realism requires attention to:

- cost,
- time,
- asymmetry,
- exposure,
- non-events,
- residual scripts,
- and binding leakage.

### 10.8 Optional Docking to MIP

Where public critique or system-level evaluation is required, PMS–SEX may dock to **Maturity in Practice (MIP)** without merging layers.

| PMS–SEX | MIP |

## 28. Final Note

PMS–SEX does not tell you what sex *should* be.

It makes visible what sexual configurations **produce** — especially where costs emerge, how drift becomes prepared, how inner framing and outer form can diverge, and why many conflicts arise not from malice but from structural opacity under time, asymmetry, non-event, and repetition.

Sexual configurations become structurally legible when:

- frames are explicit,
- non-events remain visible,
- repetition stabilizes scripts,
- asymmetry distributes cost,
- time accumulates consequence,
- distance remains operative,
- integration remains possible,
- and self-binding is handled rather than leaked.

PMS–SEX becomes structurally invalid when it:

- turns configuration language into person judgment,
- turns outer legibility into accusation,
- turns path-cost into pathology,
- turns desire into diagnosis,
- turns orientation into rank,
- turns analysis into instruction,
- turns dignity stress into humiliation,
- or turns structural readability into moral verdict.

PMS–SEX remains valid by staying within configuration-language:

> not what persons are,  
> but what configurations do over time.
