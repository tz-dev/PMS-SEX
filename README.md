## What PMSвҖ“SEX Is

PMSвҖ“SEX is:

* a **scene grammar** for explicit sexual practice: enactments, roles, frames, and non-events
* a **consequence model** for how repetition stabilizes (О‘), how costs distribute (О©), and how time makes effects durable (Оҳ)
* a framework that treats **absence / withdrawal / non-occurrence (Оӣ)** as structurally active under expectation
* a **viability-and-drift lens**: when configurations remain governable, and when drift becomes likely
* a way to discuss вҖңriskвҖқ as **praxeological structure**, not as moral verdict or psychological diagnosis
* an overlay that distinguishes **internal framing**, **outer legibility**, **publicness**, and **temporal carryover** without collapsing them

**Guiding formula:**

> **Non-clinical in stance /  
> Structural in mechanism /  
> Guardrailed in application**

## What PMSвҖ“SEX Is *Not*

PMSвҖ“SEX is not:

* a therapy framework or clinical model
* a moral doctrine, purity narrative, or normative sex ethics

PMSвҖ“SEX evaluates **configurations**, not persons.  
Critique targets **structures, trajectories, and cost handling**, not identities.

## Core Thesis

PMSвҖ“SEX argues that sexual practice becomes structurally readable because:

* **Оӣ (non-event)** is active: absence, withdrawal, hesitation, and delay can steer under expectation.
* **О‘ (attractor)** stabilizes: repetition consolidates scripts and narrows options.
* **О© (asymmetry)** distributes costs: access, exposure, obligation, exit margin, and repair load rarely remain symmetric.
* **Оҳ (temporality)** accumulates: consequences persist and reshape exit realism over time.
* **ОҰ (recontextualization)** can reframe a scene, but does not rewind accumulated structure.
* **О§ (distance)** is the decisive limiter: without practical stop-capability and meta-position, drift becomes hard to intercept.
* **ОЈ (integration)** is required where contradictory demands must be held without denial or coercive simplification.
* **ОЁ (self-binding)** is a threshold: local events and non-events can become biography-relevant, binding-relevant, or identity-relevant under time.

> Sexuality does not вҖңcostвҖқ because it is immoral.  
> It costs because it happens under bodies, frames, asymmetry, repetition, and time.

## Method Guards

PMSвҖ“SEX is valid as a PMS application only under the standard gate:

* **О§ вҖ” Distance preserved:** analysis maintains meta-position and stop-capability; no fusion into urgency, accusation, enforcement, or role-perspective.
* **Reversibility preserved:** claims remain scene-bound, revisable, and configuration-specific; no global person labels.
* **D вҖ” Dignity-in-Practice preserved:** critique avoids shaming, humiliation, diagnosis, orientation taxonomy, and person-ranking.

Two central guard sentences:

> **Internal framing does not erase outer form.**
> **Naming outer legibility does not rank persons.**

PMSвҖ“SEX may describe **path-cost**, **stabilization burden**, **disturbance**, or **configuration-level deviation**, but only as structural language. These terms do not diagnose persons, rank identities, lower dignity, or imply moral pathology.

## Inner / Outer / Public Layer Discipline

PMSвҖ“SEX distinguishes four layers that may overlap but must not be collapsed:

| Layer                  | Question                                                                                          |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Internal framing**   | How is the scene inhabited, narrated, or lived from within?                                       |
| **Outer legibility**   | What action-form is structurally readable under asymmetry, exposure, repetition, and consequence? |
| **Publicness**         | Does visibility, documentation, reputation, or circulation amplify consequences?                  |
| **Temporal carryover** | How does the scene continue across Оҳ?                                                             |

Publicness is treated as a **в–Ў extension**: it may distribute, amplify, document, repeat, or sedimentize consequences, but it does not necessarily generate the primary action-form.

## Structural Contributions

### 1. Sexuality as Consequence System

PMSвҖ“SEX reconstructs sexuality as praxis that produces:

* scripts and attractors (О‘)
* cost gradients (О©)
* trajectory effects (Оҳ)
* recontextualization pressure (ОҰ)
* distance or stop-capability stress (О§)
* binding relevance (ОЁ)

### 2. Non-Event Seriousness

Withdrawal, silence, delay, hesitation, and absence are treated as structurally active under expectation вҖ” not as вҖңnothing happened.вҖқ

### 3. Viability Without Moralizing

Functional practice is defined by **governability**, not normality:

```text
в–Ў explicit
+ О© nameable
+ Оҳ realistic
+ О§ operative
+ Оӣ tolerable
+ ОЁ handled
(+ ОЈ reachable)
= structurally governable configuration
```

### 4. Outer Legibility Without Person-Ranking

PMSвҖ“SEX can name externally readable action-forms вҖ” such as asymmetry-form, exposure-form, humiliation-form, or degradation-form вҖ” without converting that analysis into moral verdict, identity judgment, diagnosis, or person-worth claim.

### 5. Path-Cost Without Pathology

PMSвҖ“SEX may describe higher stabilization burden, disturbance, or path-cost only at the level of configuration dynamics.

> Disturbance names structural stabilization burden, not human worth.

### 6. Optional Docking to MIP

Where public critique or system-level evaluation is required, PMSвҖ“SEX may dock to **MIP** without merging layers:

* PMSвҖ“SEX maps structure.
* MIP evaluates critique legitimacy.
* Outer legibility alone does not automatically authorize public accusation.

PMSвҖ“SEX is intended for:

* structural theorists analysing sexuality without diagnosis or moral ranking
* discourse contexts where critique must be hard but non-degrading
