1. Purpose and epistemic status

This document specifies the PMS-SEX compact application profile in a technical, human-readable form. It summarizes the formal structure expressed in PMS-SEX.yaml, including model identity, guard registries, operator spine, concept registry, drift registry, viability criteria, modules, limits, analysis pipeline, example schema, case-template interface, and final profile guard.

PMS-SEX is a praxeological grammar of sexual praxis. It does not claim empirical finality about sexuality, preference, identity, orientation, biology, or personhood. Its value lies in making sexual configurations describable without collapsing them into diagnosis, morality, confession, sex truth, genital truth, practice prescription, or public punishment.

The profile reads sexuality as enacted configuration under frame, non-event, attractor formation, asymmetry, temporality, recontextualization, distance, integration, and self-binding. Its canonical PMS operator grammar remains:

Δ → ∇ → □ → Λ → Α → Ω → Θ → Φ → Χ → Σ → Ψ
Source paper paper basis

PMS-SEX — From Impulse to Self-Binding: A Praxeological Grammar of Sexuality (Δ–Ψ)

PMS-SEX remains valid by staying within what it can responsibly say: not what persons are, but what configurations do over time. It permits hard structural claims about cost, drift, outer legibility, publicness, body/boundary coding, genital sovereignty drift, after-scene dignity, and responsibility handling without converting those claims into person-status judgments.

Core formula scope discipline

PMS-SEX makes sexual configurations structurally criticizable without making persons sexually confessional, diagnosable, rankable, or morally capturable.

7. Layer separation and misreading guards

PMS-SEX distinguishes internal framing, outer legibility, publicness, body/boundary layer, and temporal carryover. These layers may overlap, but none may erase the others.

Publicness Does visibility, documentation, circulation, audience, or reputation modify consequences? Treating publicness as either the whole issue or as irrelevant.

Non-collapse rule layer discipline

A scene may be internally framed one way, externally readable another way, publicly circulated in a third way, body-bound in a fourth way, and temporally carried in a fifth way. PMS-SEX reads the relation between layers without using any one layer as total explanation.

8. Sexual cost topology and asymmetry-specific responsibility

Sexual cost topology is the scene-bound distribution of bodily, temporal, exposure-related, reputational, binding-related, exit-related, publicness-related, body/boundary-related, access-related, and responsibility-related costs. It is not a score, not a person measure, not a maturity measure, and not a dignity measure.

Asymmetry is not the problem. False coordination of real asymmetry is the problem. PMS-SEX does not require identical burdens, identical role tasks, or symmetrical content. It requires that unequal burdens not be hidden, moralized, externalized, or converted into authority.

Sexual reciprocity under real asymmetry does not require equal burdens. It requires that unequal burdens not be externalized.

8.4 Asymmetry-specific responsibility

Each role-position must carry the self-binding required by its own cost, power, exposure, exit, access, and externalization profile. This is a scene-bound responsibility reading under Ω/Θ, not a hierarchy of persons.

PMS-SEX is intentionally bounded. It reads sexual configurations as structures over time. It does not replace psychology, therapy, law, ethics, empirical research, phenomenology, history, sociology, discourse analysis, community knowledge, or MIP.

14.1 Non-capture conditions
